About Me

Welcome! I'm a software engineer, data engineer, and former educator who enjoys building systems that help people make sense of information and take action with confidence.

My path into technology wasn't a traditional one. Before working professionally in software and data, I spent more than a decade teaching mathematics, computer science, engineering, and robotics. If those years taught me anything, it's how to simplify complexity, communicate clearly, and build technology around the people who use it. Whether I'm designing a dashboard, developing a website, or helping someone bring an idea to life online, I'm most interested in creating tools that feel useful, approachable, and genuinely reflective of the people they're built for.

Today I bring that same mindset to software development. Whether I'm building data pipelines, developing web applications, creating analytics dashboards, or exploring new technologies, I'm most motivated by work that turns complexity into clarity.

I enjoy learning new tools and understanding how things work beneath the surface. Curiosity has taken me from classrooms to data engineering, frontend development, cloud technologies, accessibility, analytics, and civic-tech projects. Each project becomes an opportunity to learn something new while creating something useful.

I hold a degree in Applied Mathematics from San Diego State University and have worked across education, data engineering, web development, and nonprofit technology initiatives. My experiences have reinforced a simple belief: good technology should be dependable, understandable, and built with purpose.

When I'm not building software, you'll usually find me hiking, exploring new ideas, contributing to community-focused projects, or spending time with family.
